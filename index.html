Tableau Projects

This Tableau project analyzes Air BnB data and illustrates the findings in a easy to understand and visually appealing manner. The Dashboard illustrates answers to a variety of potential questions a stakeholder may ask, the Bar chart illustrates the average price per night for a 1 bedroom up to a 6 bedroom location. as you can see the more bedrooms equals the more the host can charge per night. In the table next to the bar chart it highlights the number of listings based on the number of bedrooms. the most common number of bedrooms is by far 1 bedroom, as the bedrooms number gets larger the number of listings decrease significantly. The Map shows the breakdown of the average price per zip code. It shows in which states are charging more and less on average per booking. The bar chart next to the map highlights the same information in a bar chart to reinforce the analysis on which states are charging more per booking. Finally the Revenue for year is a line chart that illustrates the revenue throughout the year showing the peak times and low times to give the stakeholder an idea when to list their home on Air BnB to acheive the maximum revenue possible. The graph shows that during winter the revenue is on the low end and peaks in June. The summer months show strong revenue at a steady pace which is a clear indication more people are renting rooms during the summer.

Microsoft Excel Dashboard

In this project I analayzed bicycle sales data in the world based on a variety of different variables such as marital status, education level, the georgraphical location, etc. I first cleaned the data to make sure there were no duplicates, i trimmed any white space, adjusted the columns, I created buckets for the age ranges to make it easir to visualize in a chart format. I also created buckets for the commuting distance again to make it easier to visualize in chart format. I also created a few pivot tables and pivot charts for quick analysis of the data, the first pivot tables analyzes the average income of male and female's who either purchased a bike or did not purchase a bike. For both male and female the higher the income resulted in more purchases of a bike than not. the second pivot table analyszed the commute distance for people who either purchased a bike or not. The data shows that for shorter distances more people purchased bikes than for longer commutting distance. The final pivot table analyzes the age vs purchased a bike or not. The results show middle age were the ones to purchase the most bikes vs adolescent and older individuals. The final sheet is a dashboard that brings together all the above charts and data into a centralized easy to understand and visually appelaing space. Included in the dasboard are slicers that allow the stakeholder to manipulate the data based on a variety of different combinations, such as gender, region and marital status.
